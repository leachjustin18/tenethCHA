INFANT
Sources of Stress
The hospital environment (e.g., bright lights, loud noises, change in room temperature), separation from the parents/caregiver, sleep disruption.
Stress Response
Crying, hyperactivity and nonspecific behaviors, such as jitteriness, agitation, elevated vital signs, breathing irregularities and feeding intolerances.
TODDLER
Sources of Stress
Seperation from the parents/caregiver, changes in the environment, painful procedures (may perceive that the procedures are intended as punishment).
Stress Response
Hyperactivity, loud crying or screaming, regression (e.g., previsously toliet-trained toddlers may lose bladder control), withdrawal.
PRESCHOOL
Sources of Stress
Seperation from the parents/caregiver, the unkown (e.g., fear of the dark or hospital personnel), multiple invasive procedures (may view procedures as punshment for previous bad behavior and may fear that these procedures will result in bodily mutilation).
Stress Response
Regression, assigning human characteristics to inanimate objects, withdrawal, asking questions or repaeatign phrases over and over, such as "I want my mommy!" or "Why?"
SCHOOL-AGE
Sources of Stress
Seperation from daily routine, loss of control, immobility and bodily injury.
Stress Response
Regression, Sadness, denial of fears and act "grown up," sleep disturbances.
ADOLESCENTS
Sources of Stress
Seperation from peer groups, loss of control and identity, loss of privacy.
Stress Response
Withdrawal, regression, sleep disturbances, deny obvious pain and discomfort, especially if peers are present..
